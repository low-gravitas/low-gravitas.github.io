Fonts that age well

Typography is the slowest-changing layer of a design system. Choose a face that won't embarrass you in five years — the wrong choice dates everything else around it. Look for typefaces that have already survived decades.

What "aging well" means

Three things: stylistic restraint (no trend-chasing quirks), strong information architecture (a real weight range, proper italics), and institutional support (the foundry will still be here in ten years).

A good typeface is silent. It holds the content up without being the content.

The shortlist

  • Fraunces — variable serif with genuine warmth. Works as display and body.
  • IBM Plex — corporate-grade neutral sans with matching mono and serif.
  • Inter — ubiquitous but safe, and it'll still be safe in 2035.

Caveats

Avoid display faces masquerading as body faces. A 48px headline is not a 16px paragraph.
